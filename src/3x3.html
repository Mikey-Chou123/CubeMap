3×3 Roadmap

CFOP

The CFOP method (Cross, F2L, OLL, PLL) is the most widely used speedsolving method. It’s structured, algorithm-based, and emphasizes memorization and efficiency — the go-to for most world-class cubers.

  • Cross: Build a cross on one face as a foundation.
  • F2L: Solve corner-edge pairs for the first two layers.
  • OLL: Orient the last layer with algorithms.
  • PLL: Permute all pieces to finish the solve.

Best suited for: algorithm lovers and competitive speedcubers.

Roux

The Roux method focuses on block-building and intuition. Instead of memorizing hundreds of algorithms, solvers rely on spatial reasoning, freedom, and move efficiency.

  • First Blocks Build 1×2×3 blocks on the left.
  • Second Block Build another 1×2×3 blocks this time on the right.
  • CMLL: Solve the top corners while preserving the blocks.
  • LSE: Finish the remaining edges using minimal moves.

Best suited for: creative thinkers who prefer intuition over algorithms.
