Roux Method

The Roux method focuses on building efficiency and flexibility through intuitive blockbuilding rather than heavy algorithm use. It’s divided into four main stages that flow naturally and reward lookahead, precision, and creativity.

First Block

Build a 1×2×3 block on one side of the cube, forming the foundation of the Roux method. Focus on efficiency and lookahead to reduce unnecessary moves early on.

Second Block

Construct the second 1×2×3 block on the opposite side using intuitive moves. This step completes most of the cube’s lower section without relying on algorithms.

CMLL

Solve all four last-layer corners in a single algorithm, both orienting and permuting them simultaneously. There are 42 possible cases, but learning even a few can greatly improve speed.

LSE

Finish the solve by orienting and permuting the remaining six edges. This stage emphasizes turning efficiency and flow rather than memorization.

The Roux method rewards creativity and control — once you master its flow, every solve feels fluid and efficient.
